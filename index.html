Project_03

Super Bowl X Netflix Pitch

My first animatic ever. The assignment was to create a Super Bowl commercial with a product and a celebrity, but I asked if I could make a commercial for the Super Bowl instead. So I pitched a Netflix sponsorship for Super Bowl LIX featuring The Queen's Gambit. The funny thing is I don't watch football and I've never seen the show. It was just the first thing that came to mind and I rolled with it. The animatic follows the actual order of a football game and a chess match side by side, which is one of my favorite details. Everything was hand drawn in Photoshop and animated in After Effects.

Tools: After Effects, Mixed Media, Photoshop

Personnel File

Armand Slaton

3D Generalist & Motion Artist

I'm a 3D Generalist currently studying at Gnomon School of VFX and working as a Production Assistant at Statik Creative. My very first sculpture, a character study of Jinx from Arcane, was acquired by Riot Games. First one I ever made. I didn't even win best of term for it, but apparently the right people were watching.

I wouldn't exactly say I followed a typical path into this industry. At 16, I walked into Lightbox Expo alone with no connections and talked my way into a conversation with Sony recruiters. I've always believed that if you show up and put yourself in the room, the work will speak for itself.

I've assisted on commercial photography shoots at Statik Creative, handling equipment and on set logistics. At school, I contributed to yearbook publishing, where not a single photo or word could afford to be out of place. That same discipline drives how I name files, structure projects, and hand off assets.

I approach every project like it's going to be seen by the studio I want to work for, because now it is. I want to make work that inspires people the same way the work I admire has inspired me. Whether I'm building environments in Unreal, compositing in After Effects, or sculpting by hand, the standard is always: give it everything.

The Journey

2023

Gnomon Summer Camp

First exposure to professional level VFX training. Took the Gnomon summer program and knew immediately this was the path.

2023

Lightbox Expo — Age 16

Attended Lightbox Expo alone at 16 with one goal: meet Sony. Followed a gut feeling to switch lines and ended up in a conversation with Chris Leahy, executive director of global talent acquisition at Sony Pictures Animation, who introduced me to her colleague at DreamWorks and took me backstage after the Spider Verse panel to meet Patrick O'Keefe and Mike Lasker.

2024

Film Festival Winner

"You're Not Alone" wins Best of Show at the Hart District Film Festival. A short film exploring paranoia and sleep paralysis that I wrote, directed, and edited. I had no idea that submitting a film to my school's festival would set everything else in motion.

2024

Lightbox Expo — Return

Returned to Lightbox Expo and reconnected with Sony recruiters. Met the storyboard artist from Into the Spider Verse. What started as a cold introduction the year before became a real professional relationship.

2025

Gnomon Acceptance

Admitted to the Gnomon School of VFX through a competitive portfolio submission. Began training in 3D modeling, compositing, and environment design under industry professionals. Walking the halls covered in movie posters, almost every one made by a Gnomon alum.

2025

Acquired by Riot Games

Within my first 10 week term at Gnomon, my Jinx sculpture, my very first sculpture ever, was acquired by Riot Games. I didn't win best of term for it. But the right people noticed anyway.

2026

What's Next

Actively pursuing studio opportunities in VFX and animation, expanding into Unreal Engine environments, and refining my pipeline workflow. I'm still figuring out exactly what I want to specialize in, whether that's sculpting, 3D modeling, or cinematography, but the goal has never changed. I'll work as hard as I have to and wait as long as I need to. I will get there.
